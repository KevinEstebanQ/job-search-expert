# Cover Letter Style Guide

This file tells the AI drafter how to write your cover letters.
Fill it in honestly — the more specific, the better the output.

---

## Voice & Tone

[Describe how you naturally write. Examples:]
- Concise and direct — no filler phrases
- Confident but not arrogant
- Conversational, not stiff or corporate
- Technical but accessible — I explain what I built, not just that I built it

---

## What to Emphasize

[List the things you most want highlighted in cover letters:]
- [E.g. "Specific projects from my portfolio that match the job's stack"]
- [E.g. "My ability to learn new technologies quickly"]
- [E.g. "Hands-on experience with X even if it's from coursework or personal projects"]
- [E.g. "My passion for backend systems and scalable architecture"]

---

## What to Avoid

[Phrases or approaches you don't want:]
- Clichés: "I am passionate about...", "I am a quick learner", "synergy"
- Overly long intros that don't get to the point
- Claiming expertise in things I'm still learning
- Mentioning salary expectations

---

## Opening Style

[How do you like to open a cover letter? Examples:]
- Lead with a specific thing about the company that genuinely interests me
- Lead with the most relevant project I've built
- Lead with a concise value statement: what I do + what I'm looking for

---

## Length

[Target length:]
- 2–3 paragraphs max
- Under 300 words preferred
